# 1. Purpose

This document defines how Artificial Intelligence and deterministic business logic cooperate within the platform.

The system intentionally separates:

- AI-powered language understanding
- Deterministic pricing calculations

This separation ensures:

- Reliability
- Explainability
- Repeatability
- Human oversight

AI assists with understanding customer requests, while pricing decisions remain fully deterministic.

---

# 2. Design Philosophy

The platform follows three fundamental principles.

---

## AI Understands Language

AI is responsible for:

- Reading customer emails
- Understanding intent
- Extracting structured information
- Generating draft emails

AI does **not** make business decisions.

---

## Software Calculates Business Logic

Pricing calculations are implemented in Python.

Business rules include:

- Unit pricing
- Quantity calculations
- Discount rules
- Margin calculations
- Tax calculations

Identical inputs must always produce identical outputs.

---

## Humans Approve Business Decisions

No quotation is sent automatically.

Operators review:

- AI extraction
- Pricing results
- Customer information
- Generated email draft

Human approval is required before workflow completion.

---

# 3. Responsibility Separation

| Component | Responsibility |
|-----------|----------------|
| OpenAI | Language understanding |
| Validation Engine | Data validation |
| Pricing Engine | Business calculations |
| Human Reviewer | Final approval |

Each component performs a single responsibility.

# 4. AI Workflow

The AI workflow begins immediately after an RFQ email is received.

```text
Customer Email
        │
        ▼
OpenAI
        │
        ▼
Structured JSON
        │
        ▼
Validation
        │
        ▼
Pricing Engine
```

The AI output always passes through deterministic validation before business processing continues.

---

# 5. AI Input

The AI model receives:

- Email subject
- Email body
- Customer information (if available)
- Additional context (future)

Example:

```text
Subject:
Request for quotation

Body:
We need 250 units of Product A delivered before July 30.
Please include shipping costs.
```

The AI model processes only textual information.
---

# 6. AI Output

The AI service converts unstructured customer communication into structured JSON.

Example:

```json
{
  "customer_name": "John Smith",
  "company_name": "Example Corp",
  "customer_email": "john@example.com",
  "products": [
    {
      "name": "Product A",
      "quantity": 250
    }
  ],
  "delivery_date": "2026-07-30",
  "notes": "Include shipping costs."
}
```

This structured output becomes the input for downstream validation and pricing.

---

# 7. Prompt Design

The extraction prompt should produce predictable, structured responses.

## Objectives

- Extract all required RFQ fields
- Avoid hallucination
- Preserve customer intent
- Return valid JSON
- Leave unknown values empty rather than guessing

---

## Example System Prompt

```text
You are an assistant that extracts quotation requests.

Return ONLY valid JSON.

Do not invent information.

If information is missing, return null.

Extract:

- Customer Name
- Company
- Email
- Products
- Quantities
- Delivery Date
- Notes
```

Prompt instructions should emphasize correctness over completeness.

---

# 8. Validation After AI

AI output is never trusted without validation.

Validation checks include:

- Required fields
- Valid quantities
- Supported products
- Date format
- Email format

Example workflow:

```text
AI Output
      │
      ▼
Validation
      │
 ┌────┴─────┐
 │          │
 ▼          ▼
Pass      Fail
 │          │
 ▼          ▼
Pricing   Human Review
```

Invalid AI output never reaches the pricing engine.

---

# 9. Pricing Engine

The pricing engine is implemented as deterministic Python code.

Responsibilities include:

- Unit pricing
- Quantity calculations
- Discount calculations
- Tax calculations
- Margin calculations
- Final quotation totals

The pricing engine never calls an LLM.

---

---

# 12. Pricing Algorithm

The pricing engine follows a deterministic calculation sequence.

```text
Input Data
      │
      ▼
Validate Inputs
      │
      ▼
Retrieve Unit Prices
      │
      ▼
Calculate Subtotal
      │
      ▼
Apply Discounts
      │
      ▼
Calculate Tax
      │
      ▼
Calculate Final Total
      │
      ▼
Store Results
```

Every stage produces deterministic outputs.

---

# 13. Example Pricing Formula

Example pseudocode:

```python
subtotal = unit_price * quantity

discount_amount = calculate_discount(subtotal)

tax_amount = calculate_tax(subtotal - discount_amount)

total = subtotal - discount_amount + tax_amount
```

The exact business rules may vary by organization, but the implementation should remain deterministic and testable.

# 16. Failure Handling

## AI Failure

Examples:

- Invalid JSON
- Missing fields
- API timeout

Recovery:

- Retry
- Manual review

---

## Validation Failure

Examples:

- Missing quantity
- Invalid product
- Invalid email

Recovery:

- Manual correction

---

## Pricing Failure

Examples:

- Missing pricing rule
- Invalid tax configuration

Recovery:

- Operator investigation
- Correct pricing data
- Recalculate quotation

No quotation should proceed until pricing succeeds.

|--------|-------------|

Logs should include:

- Timestamp

- Workflow ID
- Event type
- Execution duration

Structured logging simplifies debugging and operational monitoring.

# 18. AI Safety Guidelines

The AI service should operate within clearly defined boundaries.

## The AI SHOULD:

- Extract factual information from customer requests.
- Preserve the customer's original intent.
- Return structured JSON.
- Leave missing values empty (`null`) rather than guessing.
- Generate professional email drafts after approval.

---

## The AI SHOULD NOT:

- Invent missing information.
- Modify pricing.
- Approve quotations.
- Apply discounts.
- Change business rules.
- Override deterministic calculations.

These constraints reduce hallucinations and keep business logic under deterministic control.

# 19. Design Decisions

## DD-001 AI for Language Only

**Decision**

Use AI exclusively for language understanding and content generation.

**Reason**

Language interpretation benefits from LLMs, while business calculations require deterministic behavior.

---

## DD-002 Deterministic Pricing

**Decision**

Implement pricing exclusively in Python.

**Reason**

- Repeatable outputs
- Easier testing
- Transparent business rules
- Easier debugging

---

## DD-003 Validation Before Pricing

**Decision**

Validate all extracted data before any calculation.

**Reason**

Prevents invalid inputs from propagating through the quotation process.

---

## DD-004 Human Approval Required

**Decision**

Require operator approval before generating the final customer response.

**Reason**

Maintains accountability and minimizes the risk of incorrect quotations reaching customers.

---

## DD-005 Editable AI Output

**Decision**

Allow operators to modify AI-extracted information and generated drafts.

**Reason**

Human reviewers remain the final authority on business communications and quotation accuracy.

---

# 20. Conclusion

The AI & Pricing Engine architecture intentionally separates probabilistic AI capabilities from deterministic business logic.

OpenAI is responsible for understanding natural language and generating structured outputs, while Python implements all pricing calculations according to explicit business rules. Every quotation passes through validation and human review before completion.

This layered approach provides a balance between automation, reliability, transparency, and operational control, forming the core intelligence of the AI-powered RFQ Operations Platform.
